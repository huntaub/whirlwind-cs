Is it a computer?

The slide rule, also known colloquially in the United States as a slipstick, is a mechanical analog computer.

What is a computer?

computer (noun): a person who computes; computist.
compute (verb): to determine by calculation; reckon; calculate
circa 1640
It is important to note that the term computer was around long before the "computers" we think about (and will study in this course) came about. This wasn't even derogatory!

Anything can be a computer.

We just have a narrow definition today.

In fact, we had "Computer Scientists" well before we had computers as well (although they were generally Mathematicians). They studied "processes," and examined what was effectively "computable."

What is a computer?

computer (noun): mechanical calculating machine

circa 1897, Babbage's machine is from 1822
The first machines that resembled the computers that we have today were mechanical since we were already so good at that. They used literal switches and gears and motors in order to compute. What was the name of the machine pictured here?

What is a computer?

computer (noun): programmable digital electronic computer

circa 1945
Finally, we start to talk about digital computers. What does it mean for something to be digital?

Why the transition?

Moving parts are:

  • Slow
  • Unreliable
  • Expensive
It is important to realize how fickle the real world is when we attempt to interact with it. Mini USB (the port on your old Motorola Razr) was rated for 5000 insertions over the lifecycle of the device. Now, micro USB (the port on your Android phone) is rated for 10000. Compare that to your processor which has billions of operations occur every second.

The Switch!

Of course, in order to compute something, we need a conditional. That is represented electrically as a switch.

Vacuum Tubes

Vacuum Tubes were the first electric switches. You may have heard of them, and they look something like the background. We see the development of vacuum tubes in 1907 by Lee De Forest (some may say that Robert von Lieben invented it in 1906).

These function almost like a valve for water. The middle grid gets a current that can slow the flow of electrons through the tube.

The Transistor

Solid State!

Finally, we see the introduction of the transistor. It was invented at Bell Labs by Bardeen, Brattain, and Shockley in 1947. They received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1956.

The Key was Semiconductors

Semiconductors allowed us to selectively control conductivity through material without gaps.
The transistor is the key active component in practically all modern electronics. Many consider it to be one of the greatest inventions of the 20th century.

Moore's Law

I am sure that you have heard of Moore's Law. It does not, in fact, mean that the speed of computers doubles every 2 years. Rather, it talks about the number of transistors on a chip. Do you think that you could fit billions of the transistors we saw just earlier inside your laptop?
