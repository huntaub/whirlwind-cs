Let's go through some examples.

Sleeping in Class

I have two variables, and I need to know if I am asleep during lecture.

  • A - Lecture is exciting.
  • B - I got enough sleep last night.

\( (\lnot A) \land (\lnot B) \)

\( \lnot (A \lor B) \)

This is called DeMorgan's Law.

Eating Chipotle

I have three variables, and I want to know if I should go eat at Chipotle.

  • A - I am hungry.
  • B - Chipotle is open.
  • C - There is food in my apartment.

\( (A \land B) \land (\lnot C) \)
