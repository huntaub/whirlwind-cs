Network Types

  1. Circuit Switched

Circuit Switched Networking

A network in which a physical circuit is used for a single connection.

Largest Circuit Switched Network: Telephones

Manual Operators - ~1878

Rotary Dial - ~1891

Touch Tone - ~1963

Circuit Switched Networking

Perks:

  1. Constant Latency
  2. Full Bandwidth Always Available

Issues:

  1. Expensive
  2. Complicated to Manage

about 10 billion

devices connected to the internet.

Packet Switched Networking

A physical circuit is shared among many different connections. (Just like USB!)

Largest Packet Switched Network: The Internet
